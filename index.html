Borrow This Itinerary

Traveling this route yourself? Take it — it's yours.

This is a real, field-planned eight-day loop that uses Cochrane as a single base camp — cheaper and calmer than staying in Banff, with every day trip under two hours' drive. Fly into Calgary (YYC) and rent a car — you'll want it every single day. The chapters below work as a template: same order, same timing, swap the buffer day for whatever the sky gives you. Three practical things to sort out before you go:

🎟️ Park passes (two different ones!)
  • Banff, Yoho & Kootenay need a Parks Canada pass — for 7+ days the annual Discovery Pass usually beats daily fees.
  • Kananaskis & Spray Valley (Grassi Lakes, Spray Lakes, Upper Kananaskis Lake) need a separate Kananaskis Conservation Pass — the federal pass does not cover them.
📅 Book these before you fly
  • Moraine Lake shuttle — private cars are banned on the road; reserve a Parks Canada shuttle or a commercial bus seat well ahead.
  • Banff Gondola — sells out in peak season; book online for 5–10% off.
  • Lodging — Cochrane and Canmore book out months ahead for September weekends.
🐻 Wildlife & bear sense
  • Carry bear spray on any real trail (Grassi, Lake Agnes, Johnston Upper Falls) and know how to use it — sold in Banff & Canmore.
  • Make noise on the trail, hike as a group, and keep 100 m from bears, 30 m from elk — that telephoto crop is worth more than a close-up.
  • Never feed wildlife; dawn and dusk are prime sighting (and caution) hours. Full guidance: Parks Canada bear safety.
